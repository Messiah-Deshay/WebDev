The Shift Theory suggests that every physical relocation of the Mona Lisa alters the fabric of history. At the European Historical Anomaly Committee (EHAC), we investigate these phenomena through rigorous study and collaborative research.

Historical Relocations of the Mona Lisa

EHAC maintains a detailed timeline tracking the painting's known movements and associated anomalies:

  • 1911: Stolen from the Louvre, returned in 1913.
  • 1939: Relocated to Italy during World War II for protection.
  • 2025: Upcoming Seventh Relocation scheduled for Fall 2025.

Understanding Shift Theory

Shift Theory proposes that reality is subtly reshaped each time the Mona Lisa changes location. EHAC researchers study the ripple effects of these events to better understand how cultural artifacts may influence history and time perception.

Ongoing Collaborations

We partner with global historians, scientists, and institutions to explore the Shift Theory. If you're interested in contributing, consider joining EHAC through our Membership page.
