Who We Are

The European Historical Anomaly Committee (EHAC) investigates unusual patterns in history, unexplained connections between world events, and the hidden details that shape our collective past. Our mission is to illuminate phenomena that challenge conventional timelines, with a special focus on the mysterious movements of the Mona Lisa.

Our Story

Founded in the 1960s by a coalition of scholars, historians, and archivists, EHAC began with a simple observation: moments of global upheaval often followed the relocation of culturally significant artifacts. These recurring anomalies formed a pattern we now call the Shift Effect.

Our Work

While the Mona Lisa remains central to our current investigations, our research extends to art, architecture, and relics that may act as "anchors" in the historical continuum. We strive to understand how these items influence collective memory, cultural evolution, and, perhaps, the structure of reality itself.

Collaboration & Community

From assembling archives to decoding lost manuscripts, our work blends academic rigor with open-minded curiosity. We welcome skeptics, believers, and the curious to join us in exploring the threads of history.
